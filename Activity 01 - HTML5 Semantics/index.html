Lorem Ipsum Overview

Lorem ipsum (/ˌlɔː.rəm ˈɪp.səm/ LOR-əm IP-səm) is a dummy or placeholder text commonly used in graphic design, publishing, and web development. Its purpose is to permit a page layout to be designed, independently of the copy that will subsequently populate it, or to demonstrate various fonts of a typeface without meaningful text that could be distracting.

Lorem ipsum is typically a corrupted version of De finibus bonorum et malorum, a 1st-century BC text by the Roman statesman and philosopher Cicero, with words altered, added, and removed to make it nonsensical and improper Latin. The first two words are the truncation of dolorem ipsum ("pain itself").

Versions of the Lorem ipsum text have been used in typesetting since the 1960s, when advertisements for Letraset transfer sheets popularized it. Lorem ipsum was introduced to the digital world in the mid-1980s, when Aldus employed it in graphic and word-processing templates for its desktop publishing program PageMaker. Other popular word processors, including Pages and Microsoft Word, have since adopted Lorem ipsum, as have many LaTeX packages, web content managers such as Joomla! and WordPress, and CSS libraries such as Semantic UI.

Who made Lorem Ipsum?

Origin: Lorem ipsum is derived from a scrambled passage from Cicero's "De finibus bonorum et malorum" (45 BC). It was repurposed as placeholder text because the scrambled Latin resembles standard written English in its distribution of letters and lengths.

More about Cicero

Cicero (born 106 bce, Arpinum, Latium [now Arpino, Italy]-died December 7, 43 bce, Formiae, Latium [now Formia]) was a Roman statesman, lawyer, scholar, and writer who vainly tried to uphold republican principles in the final civil wars that destroyed the Roman Republic. His writings include books of rhetoric, orations, philosophical and political treatises, and letters. He is remembered in modern times as the greatest Roman orator and the innovator of what became known as Ciceronian rhetoric.

What is Lorem Ipsum all about?

The lorem ipsum text itself doesn't mean anything coherent - it's essentially nonsensical placeholder text. It became the printing and typesetting industry's standard dummy text starting in the 1500s, when an unknown printer scrambled a passage from Cicero to create a specimen book of type fonts.

The reason it's used is practical: it has a fairly normal distribution of letters and word lengths that resembles actual readable English text. This makes it useful for:

  • Showing how a design looks with text before the real content is ready
  • Avoiding distraction from the design itself (since meaningless text doesn't draw the reader's attention)

So while it has classical Latin roots, lorem ipsum itself is just gibberish used as a placeholder. The most common version starts with "Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit..." though even these words are corrupted from the original Latin.

Quick Notes & Technical Summary

  • <header> - The page header contains <h1> and a site-level <nav>. It's used to identify the page and show the navigation.
  • <nav> - Implemented as an unordered list (<ul>) of links that target section ids for in-page navigation and screen-reader clarity.
  • <main> - The primary content container for this document; all page content is nested inside it to create a single main landmark.
  • <section> - Used to group thematic content such as the Overview, Main content, and Full Text (each section has a descriptive heading).
  • <article> - The "Featured Story" ("Who made Lorem Ipsum?") is an independent, self-contained article that could be syndicated or used alone.
  • <figure> / <figcaption> - Wraps an image and its caption; keeps media and description together as a single, semantic block.
  • <details> / <summary> - Adds collapsible content for additional information (example: "More about Cicero"). Screen-readers handle these natively.
  • <mark> - Used for in-line highlights (e.g., origin label) to draw attention to short, relevant text without semantic confusion.
  • <time> - Used for datetimes. This page shows a header <time> (created/static) and a footer <time> for the last refreshed timestamp.
  • <aside> - Tangential information (like these notes); placed after the main content so it doesn't interrupt the primary reading flow.
  • <footer> - Page-level metadata, contact or author info, copyright, and the <time> element all live here.
